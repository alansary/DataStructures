Definition: A queue of elemnts of type T is a finite sequence of elements
of T together with the following operations:

1. Create the queue, leaving it empty.
2. Determine whether the queue is empty or not.
3. Determine whether the queue is full or not.
4. Find the size of the queue.
5. Traverse the queue, visiting each entry.
6. Clear the queue to make it empty.
7. Retrieve the front entry in the queue, provided the queue is not empty.
8. Append (inqueue) a new entry onto the top of the queue, provided the
   queue is
   not full.
9. Serve (and remove (dequeue)) the front entry from the queue, provided
   the queue is not empty.
